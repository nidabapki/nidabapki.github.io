Nidaba

Nidaba is a distributed scalable naming system that can be used as a DNS, an an authentication system or as a certificate authority. Nidaba is based on the Bitcoin protocol and similar to Namecoin, but has some distinguished properties. You can find the details in the whitepaper.

Why do I chose Nidaba to be the name?

N.B.
After the whitepaper was timestamped I made some minor changes to the core ideas of Nidaba: added[1] proof of stake to resists to 51%-attack and got rid[2] of penny auction.

Nidaba vs. certificate authority

Current web is based on trust to the certificates authorities. If any is compromised then a malefactor can steal one's identity and organise a man-in-the-middle attact. Of cause a CA can compromise itself by a request of the government agencies. Nidaba is based on idea that you trust no one.
